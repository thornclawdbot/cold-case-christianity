# The Big Thing About the Big Bang

**Original URL:** https://coldcasechristianity.com/writings/the-big-thing-about-the-big-bang/
**Author:** J. Warner Wallace
**Type:** Article/Essay
**Length:** ~5,800 characters

---

## Summary

Wallace argues that Christians should embrace Big Bang Cosmology as evidence FOR God's existence, not against it. The key insight: if everything came from nothing at a fixed point in the past, this supports the Genesis creation account.

**Key Points:**
1. **Big Bang supports creation from nothing** - The theory proposes the universe had a beginning, consistent with Genesis.
2. **Christian concerns are addressable** - Big Bang ≠ Naturalism ≠ Evolution ≠ Specific Age.
3. **The "big thing" about the Big Bang** - Everything came from nothing at a specific point in time.
4. **Naturalists are haunted by this** - They now seek alternatives to avoid the creation implication.

---

## Key Arguments

**On Why Christians Should Embrace Big Bang:**
"Big Bang Cosmology proposes that everything in the universe came from nothing at a fixed point in the distant past. This point of origin (a cosmological singularity) is entirely consistent with the Christian view of God's creation as described in Genesis 1."

**On What Big Bang Does NOT Require:**
1. Scientific Naturalism (science can show evidence of God)
2. Darwinian Evolution (separate from cosmology)
3. A Specific Age of the Universe (evidence points to "something from nothing" but not exact timing)

**On Evidence for "Something from Nothing":**
- Second Law of Thermodynamics
- Expansion of the universe
- Cosmic Radiation Echo
- Philosophy of infinite regress

## Why This Matters

Naturalists try to avoid the creation implication of Big Bang. The "big thing" about the Big Bang is simply this: **everything had a beginning**. This points to a Creator.

## Quote
